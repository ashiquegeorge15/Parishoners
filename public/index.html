A Journey of Faith and Community

Our Story

St. Thomas Mar Thoma Church, Bangalore started as a small prayer group in the early 2000s. Through God's grace and the dedication of its members, it has grown into a vibrant spiritual community. The congregation currently worships at the National Public School premises, fostering fellowship and spiritual growth among its members.

Our church continues to serve as a spiritual home for Mar Thoma families in Bangalore, providing worship services, Sunday School education, and various spiritual organizations that cater to different age groups and interests.

Mar Thoma Church History

A Heritage of Faith Since AD 52

Apostolic Foundation

St. Thomas, the Apostle of Jesus Christ, arrived in Cranganore (Kodungallur) in AD 52. He established seven churches and spread the Gospel throughout the Malabar coast, laying the foundation for Christianity in India.

Reformation Movement

The Mar Thoma Church emerged from a reformation movement within the Malankara Church in the 19th century, led by Abraham Malpan. This movement emphasized biblical teachings and introduced worship services in the vernacular language.

Modern Era

Today, the Mar Thoma Church continues its mission with the motto "Lighted to Lighten," maintaining its unique identity as an Episcopal Church that combines Orthodox traditions with Protestant principles.
